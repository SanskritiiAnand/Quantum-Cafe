### Brewing quantum chemistry into music.

> *What would brewing coffee sound like if the recipe were interpreted through a quantum system?*

Quantum Café is a computational sonification experiment that sits somewhere between quantum chemistry, quantum computing and generative music.

The project takes the frontier molecular orbitals of a caffeine molecule, encodes them into a parameterized quantum circuit whose parameters are influenced by a V60 pour-over recipe, simulates the resulting quantum state, and finally translates that state into a multi-track musical composition.

The result is not "music generated by a quantum computer."

Instead, it is an exploration of whether chemically meaningful quantities can be transformed into musical structure through a quantum representation.

---

## Why this project?

Most sonification projects map numbers directly to sound.

Quantum Café deliberately inserts a quantum system between the scientific data and the music.

Instead of saying

```
Chemistry → Music
```

the project asks

```
Chemistry
      ↓
Parameterized Quantum Circuit
      ↓
Quantum State
      ↓
Musical Composition
```

The quantum state acts as an intermediate representation rather than a source of randomness.

# Why caffeine?

Coffee begins with chemistry.

The project represents caffeine using six frontier molecular orbitals.

- HOMO-2
- HOMO-1
- HOMO
- LUMO
- LUMO+1
- LUMO+2

Each orbital stores two quantities:

- orbital energy (Hartree)
- geometry-derived phase factor

Instead of choosing an arbitrary number of qubits, the circuit contains one qubit for every frontier orbital.

For caffeine,

```
6 frontier orbitals
        ↓
6 qubits
```

The chemistry therefore determines the size of the quantum system.

---

# Where does the coffee recipe come in?

The V60 recipe isn't simply metadata.

Its brewing parameters modify the quantum circuit itself.

Current brewing parameters include

- coffee dose
- water mass
- brewing temperature
- bloom time
- total brew time
- pour rate

Two of these directly influence the circuit.

### Temperature

Orbital energies are first normalized,

\[
E_{norm}=\frac{E-E_{min}}{E_{max}-E_{min}}
\]

and then converted into an Ry rotation angle,

\[
\theta
=
\pi
E_{norm}
\left(\frac{T}{100}\right)
\]

Higher brewing temperatures therefore rotate every qubit further around the Bloch sphere.

---

### Bloom phase

Each orbital contains a geometry-derived phase factor.

The bloom stage determines the phase rotation

\[
\phi
=
\text{phase factor}
\times
\frac{\text{bloom time}}
{\text{total brew time}}
\]

The bloom therefore changes how the quantum state evolves before entanglement.

# The role of the Parameterized Quantum Circuit

The parameterized quantum circuit is the heart of the project.

It performs three distinct jobs.

## 1. Encoding

The molecular orbitals become quantum information.

```
Orbital energy
        ↓
      Ry(θ)

Phase factor
        ↓
      Rz(φ)
```

---

## 2. Correlation

Neighbouring frontier orbitals are entangled using CNOT gates.

```
q0 ──■────
     │
q1 ──X──■─
        │
q2 ─────X─
```

This creates correlations between orbitals instead of treating each one independently.

---

## 3. Transformation

After simulation,

the statevector

\[
|\psi\rangle
\]

is converted into measurable probabilities

\[
P_i
=
|\psi_i|^2
\]

These probabilities become the musical vocabulary of the composition.

---

# How quantum becomes music

The project never maps one orbital directly to one note.

Instead,

the quantum probability distribution determines how the music evolves.

| Quantum Feature | Musical Interpretation |
|-----------------|-----------------------|
| Probability distribution | Relative importance of notes |
| Highest-probability basis states | Melodic note selection |
| Dominant quantum state | Bass note |
| Three strongest states | Sustained pad |
| Brew curve | Phrase density |
| Probability | MIDI velocity |
| Probability | Note duration |
| Late-stage melody | Flute response |

Every bar is therefore influenced by the evolving quantum state rather than a fixed melody.

---

# Musical translation

Each musical event is generated from the simulated probability distribution.

### Velocity

\[
v
=
40
+
35P
+
30B
\]

where

- \(P\) is the basis-state probability
- \(B\) is the brew curve

Higher-probability quantum states produce stronger musical accents.

---

### Duration

\[
d
=
0.75q
+
1.10Pq
\]

where

- \(q\) is one quarter note.

More probable quantum states sustain longer notes.

---

### Brew curve

The composition itself follows

\[
B=\sin(\pi x)
\]

where \(x\) is the progress through the piece.

This creates three musical stages:

Early brew
- sparse melody
- more silence

Extraction
- increased note density
- stronger dynamics

Finish
- reduced activity
- calmer texture

The piece therefore follows the rhythm of a pour-over rather than maintaining constant complexity.

---

# Why a pentatonic scale?

The melody is restricted to an ambient morning-inspired pentatonic scale.

This allows the quantum probabilities to move freely while avoiding harsh dissonance.

Instead of forcing musical rules afterwards,

the harmonic space itself is constrained so that stochastic note selection remains coherent.

---

# Instruments

The generated MIDI contains five independent voices.

- Piano
- Bass
- Pad
- Harp
- Flute

Each instrument plays a different role within the evolving quantum texture rather than duplicating the melody.

---

# Current limitations

Despite its inspiration, Quantum Café is still a classical workflow.

The quantum circuit is simulated using Qiskit Statevector simulation.

No quantum hardware is used during composition.

The MIDI generation, orchestration and rendering are entirely classical.

The project therefore explores how quantum representations can influence musical structure rather than demonstrating quantum computational advantage.

---

# Future directions

Several natural extensions are possible.

- Execute the circuit on real Quantum hardware.
- Replace handcrafted orbital parameters with electronic structure calculations.
- Incorporate molecular Hamiltonians instead of simplified orbital descriptors.
- Use variational quantum circuits whose parameters are learned rather than fixed.
- Treat hardware noise as compositional material rather than an implementation artifact.
- Sonify molecular dynamics instead of a single molecular snapshot.
- Explore other molecules and brewing methods as entirely different musical "recipes."

# Running

```bash
python main.py
```

The program

1. builds the parameterized quantum circuit,
2. simulates the quantum state,
3. extracts quantum features,
4. composes a multi-track score,
5. exports a MIDI file.

The MIDI can then be rendered using any General MIDI synthesizer or digital audio workstation.

# Why "Quantum Café"?

Because brewing coffee is already a sequence of carefully controlled physical transformations.

Quantum Café imagines one additional step.

Instead of flowing directly from chemistry to flavour,

the coffee first passes through a quantum state,

and only then becomes music.
